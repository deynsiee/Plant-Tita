5 Herbs You Can Grow At Home

Chives

Description

Green chives have a flavor that is somewhat similar to onion. They belong to the Allium genus, which also contains leeks, garlic, and onions. Allium vegetables have been grown for centuries because of their distinctively strong aromatic flavors in food and their therapeutic benefits.

Uses/Benefits

Vitamin K, a vital element in maintaining bone density, is abundant in chives. Although vitamin K is frequently used as an osteoporosis treatment in some parts of Asia, scientists are currently investigating whether vitamin K supplements are effective in treating the condition.Choline and folate are both present in chives. Each of these elements has a relationship with improving memory functions on its own. According to studies, adults who consume more foods high in choline perform better on memory tests, while those with low choline levels appear to be more susceptible to Alzheimer's disease.

Lemongrass

Description

Cymbopogon citratus, or most commonly known as lemongrass is a perennial grass in the Poaceae family, is grown for its flavor-enhancing, fragrant leaves and stalks. The grass has several stiff stems, slender, blade-like leaves that droop toward the tips, and it grows in dense clumps.

Uses/Benefits

Some bacteria and yeast may be less likely to grow if lemongrass is present. In addition, lemongrass is thought to contain compounds that reduce fever, lower blood sugar and cholesterol levels, stimulate the uterus and menstrual flow, and have antioxidant properties.

Celery

Description

A celery stalk is typically made up of a number of long, curved sections that are joined together near the plant's base, just above the root, and where the plant enters the soil. From that point up to the crown, they grow separately and are only connected at the very bottom. Has long, cylindrical stalks with notched, yellowish-green leaves that are lobed and toothed are connected at the base to crisp, fleshy, smooth-ribbed leaf stems.

Uses/Benefits

A single stalk of celery contains at least 12 different types of antioxidant nutrients in addition to vitamin C, beta carotene, and flavonoids. Additionally, it's a fantastic source of phytonutrients, which have been demonstrated to lessen inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, cells, blood vessels, and organs.

Basil

Description

The Lamiaceae family of mint includes the annual herbaceous plant known as basil. It has square stems with oppositely growing leaves, rounded, slightly cupped leaves, and leaves that curve to form a point at the tip. Although some varieties have leaves that are reddish or purplish, the leaves are typically light green.

Uses/Benefits

In addition to treating worm infections, warts, and head colds, basil is also used to treat intestinal gas, nausea, and kidney problems. Snake and insect bites are also treated with it. For blood circulation and to start the flow of breast milk, women will occasionally use basil before and after giving birth.

Peppermint

Description

In addition to its stalked, smooth, dark green leaves and blunt, oblong clusters of pinkish lavender flowers, peppermint also has square stems. Stolons on the plant allow it to spread quickly, as with other mints.

Uses/Benefits

Mint has a long history of use as a herbal remedy for settling upset stomachs, reducing stress and anxiety, and encouraging sound sleep. For a long time, people have believed that drinking peppermint tea is a great way to calm an upset stomach and relieve indigestion, gas, and cramps.
