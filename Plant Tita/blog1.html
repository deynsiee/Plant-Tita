5 Easy Houseplants for Starters

Spider Plant

Botanical Name: Chlorophytum comosum

Description

Produce a rosette of long, thin, arched green or white-variegated foliage. These easy-to-grow houseplants, which were popular in Victorian-era households, look especially nice in a hanging basket.

Care Tips

Place the plant in a room with bright to moderate light and a temperature that is comfortable for everyone. Keep the soil moist but not watery. In the spring and summer, once per week watering is sufficient; in the winter, allow the soil to dry out more between waterings.

Dumb Cane

Botanical Name: Dieffenbachia amoena

Description

A plant with straight stem, simple and alternate leaves with white spots and flecks that make it appealing as indoor foliage. If given enough light, this fast-growing plant can reach 2 feet in height within a year of planting a rooted cutting. Although the term "dumb cane" has lost favor as a disparaging term, it was given to the plant because it contains toxins that can impair speech. It is extremely poisonous to humans, dogs, and cats.

Care Tips

The Dumb Cane plant prefers direct or indirect sunlight, as well as partial shade. It enjoys the presence of moisture in the air. Throughout the year, keep your Dumb Cane plant in an area with a 60% humidity level. Keep the top inch of soil moist but not wet. It can withstand missed watering, but prolonged watering neglect can cause its leaves to wither.

Golden Pothos

Botanical Name: : Epipremnum aureum

Description

Also known as devilâ€™s ivy, is a low-maintenance evergreen perennial houseplant. It is sought after for its glossy, green or variegated foliage on cascading stems. This is ideal for hanging baskets.

Care Tips

Plant maintenance is simple, and problems are usually few and far between. Thrive in a warm, well-lit environment with good indirect light, ideally within a few metres of a window. Non-variegated varieties will tolerate lower light levels as long as they receive a minimum of 4-6 hours of indirect light. Depending on the light and temperature, water every 5 to 7 days. Keep the soil moist but not soggy.

Purple Shamrock

Botanical Name: Oxalis triangularis

Description

Is an unusual plant with nearly black foliage. Its leaves are actually a deep purple color. The leaves are triangular and grow in clusters of three. When disturbed or at night, the leaves close up.

Care Tips

Between waterings, allow the soil to dry halfway down. Water more frequently in brighter light and less frequently in lower light. Any humidity level will suffice. Normal room humidity is allowable.

Rubber Plant

Botanical Name: Ficus elastica

Description

Rubber plants are cultivated for their large, glossy, leathery leaves. The leaves are oblong to oval in shape, 8 to 12 inches long, and come in dark green, deep maroon, or yellow, cream, pink, or white. It is made up of many trunks and a canopy with a spherical or hemispherical shape and an irregular outline.

Care Tips

Keep the soil moist but not soggy. Wipe down the leaves with a damp cloth to keep them moist and to assist your plant in absorbing more sunlight. If you don't want to wipe down every leaf, misting is an alternative.
